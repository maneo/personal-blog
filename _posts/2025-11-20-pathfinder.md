This blog is about learning new things not only Tech related stuff 😀. So, today a few words about a project which I am very proud of... building an electric guitar.

I am a casual guitar player, but this year I came back to this activity and decided to (once again) go back to electricity 😀. It started the usual research, visiting music shops, testing guitars and... budget inflation. I am sure that you are familiar with this phenomena, as you learn (or relearn after some time) more about the domain, whether this would be running shoes, drones, bikes - you realize that there is a plethora of things/features which you REALLY NEED in this new thing. This leads to higher priced items and... budget inflation. Over the years I've learned that usually you don't need ALL THESE NECESSARY THINGS unless you are really advanced in a given subject. For hobbies which require consistency, attention and time it makes more sense to start with lower tier stuff and use more expensive stuff as a reward in a journey you are about to start.

After visiting several music stores I knew what features I really like: single coils were never my thing, most comfortable fretboard radius, dark wood on fretboard is way cooler, simplicity of regulation so volume and tone gauge is enough (bye bye Les Paul). Last but not least... color, it needs to be blue 😀.

Thankfully I came across Thomann DIY guitar kits. With a significant dose of excitement I've ordered [JA kit](https://www.thomann.pl/harley_benton_electric_guitar_kit_ja.htm) (I really like the shape + compliance with my requirements). This project had all sorts of risks associated 😀 1) I am not a DIY person, 2) zero knowledge about paints and lacqueries, 3) zero knowledge about guitar regulation. 4) several other potential ways in which I could screw this up. 

Here are some pictures documenting the process. In the aftermath, the most important thing necessary to conduct such a project is.... patience.

Painting is to a large extent an exercise of patience. Apply one layer, wait, apply 2nd layer, wait, fix errors, apply 3rd layer, wait. Then wait some more for the paint to harden, apply lacquer and ... wait.

![painting the body]({{site.baseurl}}/assets/images/posts/pathfinder/05_painting.jpg)


*Free AI tip of a day no one asked for*: I've watched several YT videos showing how to properly paint guitar, you can find links to three fav ones at the end of this post. When I finally got all the cans and sanding paper in place, I've barely remembered what I've learned from these videos. This is how I've discovered one cool usage of AI 😀. Perplexity has the ability to summarize and get the most important steps from YT videos, which I found very useful for videos I've already watched and needed a refresher. 

Last stage - the assembly. It was super fast (compared to other stages of the process) when you have all the pieces in place. I did that with my daughter and we had a really cool time investigating what makes the guitar - electric. 

![font selection]({{site.baseurl}}/assets/images/posts/pathfinder/06_name_selection.jpg)
![assembly part 1]({{site.baseurl}}/assets/images/posts/pathfinder/07_assembly.jpg)

I am really pleased with how it all ended. Regulation was not that hard - I am still tweaking it here and there, but it was good for playing right after assembly. Things mentioned by people in Thoman reviews like low quality tuners or low quality transducers don't bother me much with my intensity of playing (and modern sound modelers). Another thing mentioned by people - strings and cable are definitely something which you can throw away. Overall this kit is super fun not only as a toy, but also for casual playing. 

![pathfinder]({{site.baseurl}}/assets/images/posts/pathfinder/09_pathfinder.jpg)

and yeah, I've called my guitar Pathfinder... mainly because I can 😀.

What I've learned:
* A painting job is not that hard, if you have time and will to proceed despite hiccups
* Electric guitars are way more affordable and cheap than it were 25 years ago
* You don't have to spend whole load of money to get really cool instrument
* AI is great for summarizing youtube videos
* Risky shit gives a lot enjoyment if effects are positive 🤣🤣
