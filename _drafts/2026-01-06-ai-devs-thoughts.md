What I've learned: (mainly AI dev related stuff)
- AI agents are super powerful, making code creation really fast. To assure decent quality of code you need to know what you want to get, from architecture/software design/framework/language point of view. Once again my fav old truth about AI proves its validity: AI is an automation tool - you can only automate things which you understand
- When work is progressing quickly, it is much harder to say stop and review results of AI code generation. This is where professionalism comes in place (I know, a bit of handwaving, but that's the best for now)
- Most of the problems which require human intervention are related to task context (whether it is too full, or crucial things are missing)
- AI is a superb tool to learn new things or learn best practices
